Introduction and Learning About Plants

18/05/26

Hello! This is my first post, and thus it will probably set a precedent for the website going forwards. Unfortunately, I'll have to keep it brief, as I'm between exams and still trying to figure out the whole css-html website thing.

I really like the idea of a blog, but I'm (generally) quite a privacy conscious person and I feel like all the major blogging sites are bad about data collection and/or google owned. I decided to just do it myself, which, while it took a while, allows for a much larger level of customisation and expression, as well as no restrictions placed on what I can and can't post. I make the TOS now.

Over the past few weeks, between making the decision to make this website and studying (or procrastinating doing so) I've began caring for a plant. While it's a very mundane sounding thing, it's been engaging trying to keep it alive. I'm usually very bad with plants, and that past few I've had have died unceremonious deaths, but i find that it's presence serves as good motivator for everyday activities. It makes you want to open your curtains, to make sure it gets light. It reminds you to keep a routine or schedule, so you'll have time to water it. It's also not terribly anxiety inducing, because the consequence for missing a feed or water is that your schedule gets thrown off, not it's immediate starvation or death.

The species of plant I have is called a 'fittonia', more commonly known as the nerve plant due to the markings on the leaves. It's a slow growing creeper, so it's never going to flower, but I'm alright with that. I generally prefer plants that are leafy. Fittonia are unique in their behaviour when lacking water; they 'faint' and wilt, but easily and quickly recover when watered. They're drama queens, essentially. They come from the rainforests of Peru, so they like a humid climate and a high room temperature, but since my room isn't very humid I mist it instead, allowing the water to evaporate and mimic the natural humidity.

I bought a moisture metre for it because I struggle to tell when the soil is too dry. It turns out that plants have some remarkably specific care requirements, and although this is a 'beginner' houseplant it's still kind of picky. It likes water, but not too much, and sunlight, but not directly.

I decided to name my plant, so that I had a tangible emotional attachment to it, and thus I named it Audrey. Like the man-eating plant in Little Shop of Horrors. If you've never seen the film or stage show, I can't recommend it enough. It's a Howard Ashman and Alan Menken original, and I can almost guarantee that you have heard and liked something written by them. In a few ways, it's a product of it's time, but I think it's aged well enough overall that it's still a very enjoyable watch, despite some trace elements of stereotypy and coding among some of the characters. In any case, because of the pink and green colour scheme I have a visual similarity to call upon when I don't want my plant to die.

A YouTuber named reject convenience did a wonderful video on plants (that takes a dive off topic to discuss corn) and I think it's really neat, so go ahead and give it a watch if you have time.

Again, sorry for the brevity of this post, and for it's mundanity. I'm still trying to get everything up and running (and procrastinating studying), but there will be more in depth and thoughtful writing in the near future. I'll probably provide plant updates occasionally as well.
